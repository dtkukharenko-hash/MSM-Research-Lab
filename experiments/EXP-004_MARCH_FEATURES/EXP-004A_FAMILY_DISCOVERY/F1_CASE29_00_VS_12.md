# F1 Case 29 — 00:00 vs 12:00 Tempo Break

## Scope

This note continues only F1 case #29.

- Active reference candle: T4, 2023-08-03 20:00 UTC.
- T4 body: open `0.2953`, close `0.2922`, body `0.0031`.
- T4 midpoint: `0.29375`.
- T4 high: `0.2967`.
- T4 low: `0.2886`.

Question:

Why does the expert treat 2023-08-04 12:00 UTC as the movement end, while 2023-08-04 00:00 UTC already
overlapped T4 by wick, close, and body?

No other cases are analyzed. No definition is changed. No trading rule is built.

## Immediate Context Before Each Event

### Before 2023-08-04 00:00

- Bars since T4: 1.
- T4 itself is the immediately preceding candle.
- No previous UP reaction exists after T4.
- No test of whether a new DOWN candle can continue after an overlap has happened yet.
- The T4 low `0.2886` has not been retested by any post-T4 candle before 00:00.

At 00:00 close, the market has produced the first overlap of T4, but there is not yet evidence that the
downside failed after that overlap.

### Before 2023-08-04 12:00

Sequence after T4 before the 12:00 close:

1. 2023-08-04 00:00: UP body, closes above T4 midpoint but below T4 open.
2. 2023-08-04 04:00: DOWN body, but smaller than T4 (`0.0010` vs `0.0031`) and does not update T4 low.
3. 2023-08-04 08:00: small UP body, wick again reaches above T4 midpoint.
4. 2023-08-04 12:00: UP body closes above T4 open.

At 12:00 close, the market has already shown:

- a first overlap at 00:00;
- a weaker DOWN response at 04:00;
- no new low after T4;
- a second UP attempt at 08:00;
- then a large UP close above the entire T4 body.

## Answer

The causal distinction is available at the close of 2023-08-04 12:00 UTC:

1. 00:00 is the first overlap of T4 and closes only barely above T4 midpoint, still below T4 open.
2. The 04:00 DOWN response after 00:00 is weak relative to T4 and does not update T4 low.
3. 12:00 is a repeated violation of the same active T4.
4. 12:00 closes above T4 open, reclaiming the whole T4 body.
5. 12:00's body is large relative to the previous 5 candles and almost the full size of T4's body.

Therefore, the expert-end distinction is not simply "overlap > 0.5". It is:

> repeated overlap of the still-active T4 after failed downside continuation, with a large UP body closing
> above the full T4 body.

## Verdict

# DISTINCTION_FOUND

The observable difference between 00:00 and 12:00 can be stated without future data. 00:00 is an early
overlap inside T4's body. 12:00 is a repeated and stronger break of the same active reference: it closes
above T4 open after a weak failed DOWN response.
